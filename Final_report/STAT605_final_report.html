We nd that on average, the time for completing above calculation for one network is about 0.3s. So
with more than 200,000 networks, the total calculation time is more than 17 hours. So we decide to use
CHTC to help us decrease the computation time.
We split 1000 parallel jobs and each job complete above calculation for about 200 networks. Now

computing resources. We set the required memory and disk space both 1GB for each job.

However, even though two groups of networks are statistically dierent in terms of the 3-node subgraph
density, they are not dierent in practice. In fact, for networks with label 0, the mean density vector is
[0.9910 , 0.0090]. For network with label 1, the mean density vector is [0.9925 , 0.0075]. So it is hard
to classify a network based only on the 3-node subgraph density. To solve this problem, we also explore
the 4-node subgraph density. And we nd a more practical signicant results. There are in total 6 types
of connected 4-node subgraph. The 4-node subgraph density for networks with label 0 is [0.798, 0.018,
0.101, 0.001, 0.001, 0] and for networks with label 1 is [0.676, 0.023, 0.267, 0.002, 0.001, 0]. We could nd
that there are more dierences between 4-node subgraph density. And it will be easier for us to tell the
dierences between two groups of networks.
In this report, we only focus on the labels of the networks and the real meaning of the networks does
not inuence our analysis. We focus on telling the dierences between two groups of networks.
